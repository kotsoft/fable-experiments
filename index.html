Experiments with Fable

Small, self-contained graphics experiments — each one a live demo plus a write-up of the math behind it.

  • Black hole

    A Schwarzschild black hole ray-traced in a single WebGL2 fragment shader: real geodesic lensing, the photon ring, and a relativistic accretion disk. how it works →

  • Spinning black hole

    The Kerr metric, integrated for real: frame dragging, the D-shaped shadow, and a disk that reaches three times closer in. Sweep the spin with a slider. how it works →

  • Fall into a black hole

    A first-person Schwarzschild fall with proper-time and distant-time clocks, plus a minimap for placing yourself and planning the descent vector. how it works →

  • Fallfable: through the Kerr horizon

    Fall into a rotating black hole and keep watching: past-directed GRRT in horizon-penetrating coordinates renders the accretion disk and sky from inside the event horizon, all the way down. how it works →

  • Kerr-Schild GRRT renderer

    A WebGPU Kerr black hole renderer in progress: tetrad-launched camera rays, Hamiltonian geodesic tracing, and a tunable relativistic accretion disk.

Related resources and references

Useful background, shader craft, and physics sources behind these experiments.

  • Tracing Geodesics by Mykhailo Moroz.
  • Inigo Quilez on shader math, noise, and rendering.
  • XorDev shader experiments and write-ups.
  • Kerr-Schild Kerr metric: Kerr & Schild (1965), with the Cartesian ingoing form as presented in Visser, "The Kerr spacetime: A brief introduction" (arXiv:0706.0622).
  • ISCO and photon-orbit formulas: Bardeen, Press & Teukolsky (1972).
  • Disk temperature profile: Novikov & Thorne (1973) and Shakura-Sunyaev (1973).
  • Past-directed GRRT and Hamiltonian geodesic ray tracing: standard GRRT practice, including GRay by Chan, Psaltis & Ozel (2013) and the Interstellar DNGR paper by James, von Tunzelmann, Franklin & Thorne (2015).
  • Cauchy-horizon instability and mass inflation: Poisson & Israel (1990).
  • Blackbody RGB approximation: Tanner Helland's color-temperature curve fits.
  • ACES tonemap approximation: Krzysztof Narkowicz's fitted curve.
  • Hash/value-noise/fbm and cell-hashed starfields: common shader-art idioms, with Inigo Quilez's articles as a canonical reference.
